Common Source Outbreak

When common source outbreaks occur, affected individuals undergo exposure to one common agent. If every affected individual develops the epidemic over a single exposure and incubation process, the epidemic can be classified as a point source outbreak. Yet, if the exposure was continuous or changeable, it can be classified as a continuous outbreak or intermittent oubreak.

Propagated Outbreak

In a propagated outbreak, the disease spreads from person to person. For this reason, propagated epidemics tend to last longer than common source epidemics and could lead to consecutive waves of infection if secondary and tertiary cases develop.
